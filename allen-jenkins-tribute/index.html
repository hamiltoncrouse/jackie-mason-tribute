Why he matters

The guy you remember even when he wasn’t the star.

Allen Curtis Jenkins was born on Staten Island in 1900, came up through the stage, and signed with Warner Bros in 1932. The studio knew exactly what it had: a fast-talking, gloom-faced, funny tough guy who could punch up gangster pictures, backstage musicals, newsroom comedies, and straight drama.

Jenkins worked with the kind of stars people put on posters, but his job was different. He put grease on the gears. A scene walked in wearing a suit; he made it swagger.

Later generations may know him best as the voice of Officer Charlie Dibble on Top Cat. Perfect casting: exasperated authority, street-corner warmth, and a voice that sounded like it had been arguing with a hot dog vendor since breakfast.
